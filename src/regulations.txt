DIFFERENCES FROM REAL FORMULA ONE RACING
* We assume that there were no prohibitions for tire changes in 2005, contrary to the truth.
* We assume that the blown diffuser was never banned, contrary to the truth.
* We assume that every year of formula racing has the same post-race points distribution, contrary to the truth.
* We assume that Bridgestone is the only tire manufacturer during the era between 1998 and 2008, contrary to the truth.
* We assume that refuelling was banned and drivers starts the race with a full tank during the era between 1998 and 2008, contrary to the truth.

CAR DEVELOPMENT/PRODUCTION REGULATIONS
* In-year car development is active for all regulations terms.
* In terms of car development, there is no development limitation in each season or regulation period.
* In terms of durability updates, it is eligible at the start of the season within a -6/+6 ratio. Every 1 upgrade for durability costs 2 points for car traits. The other way around is the same.
* In terms of car weight updates, it is eligible at the start of the season within a -4/+4 ratio. Every 1 kilo light costs 2 points of car traits. The other way around is the same.
* In terms of engine performance development, it is eligible at the middle of the season within a -4/+4 ratio.
* FIA can give extra development coins at the start of the season to particular teams if they seem that fair.
* Car production cost is between 13M$ and 33M$

FINANCIAL REGULATIONS
* In terms of stopping the in-year development process and transferring future income for next year car is only available 7 races left to the season-end.
* In terms of stopping the in-year development process and transferring future income for next year car yields extra +1M in each race.
